pricing engine** where the SGM team manually configures markup pricing rules, combined with a **Multi-Armed Bandit (MAB) experimentation platform** to explore which pricing strategy variant performs best within a given experiment. While this architecture has delivered incremental gains, it suffers from limitations that cap the system's ability to find optimal prices:\n\n| # | Problem | Impact |\n|-|-|-|\n| **P1** | MAB explores within a fixed, manually defined range (e.g., {+8%, +9%, +10%}). It cannot autonomously discover that a price point outside this range (e.g., +12%) would perform better. | **Lost revenue.** The system is bounded by human intuition about what ranges are worth testing, missing potentially high-value price points. |\n| **P2** | Manual experiment iteration is slow. Each cycle takes **\\~7–10 working days in the best case** — and often longer — because it requires sequential hand-offs across Data, SGM, Product, and the Experiment platform. Progress depends on Data team ticket priority, which is shared with non-pricing workstreams. | **Slow learning loop.** Weeks can pass between \"signal available\" and \"follow-up experiment live,\" leaving uplift on the table. |\n\n---\n\n# Pricing Auto-Iteration Solution\n\n## Solution Overview\n\nThis PRD defines a closed-loop auto-iteration capability: the Pricing platform evaluates experiment signals on a schedule and **updates the MAB arm set automatically** according to iteration rules, to continuously seeking optimal pricing strategy. **Scope in this PRD is strictly signal-driven iteration** — i.e., triggers derived from the experiment's own performance data, not external business conditions (seasonality, supplier rate changes, etc.).\n\n\n\nThe auto-iteration mechanism is built ***on top of*** current MAB capabilities. Users need to setup a normal MAB first, and then add the auto-iteration rules to the MAB.\n\n<grid>\n<column width-ratio=\"0.465677\">\nWhat is defined in MAB experiment:\n- Experiment audience (segmentation)\n- Success metric and Guardrail metrics\n- Overall experiment duration \n- The initial pricing treatment of each variant (the \"Arms\")\n</column>\n<column width-ratio=\"0.534323\">\nWhat is defined in Auto-iteration on top of an MAB:\n- Iteration trigger condition - When to update the arms\n- Iterated pricing stance (\"arms\") - How to update the arms\n- Iteration duration\n</column>\n</grid>\n\n## 2.1 System Interaction\n\n<whiteboard token=\"Tt8Pw7bJJhjN69blT4Dl9pXXg5g\"></whiteboard>\n\n## 2.2 Operation Workflow\n\n<whiteboard token=\"D69QwtafLhMNr2bTKGJluyVsgcg\"></whiteboard>\n\n## 2.3 Iteration Rules\n\nEach rule is defined by a **trigger condition** (against the experiment metrics), an **action** (how to produce the next arm set), and pricing **boundaries**. Cases:\n\n- **Seasonal demand changes** — when peak season arrives, demand surges and customers tolerate higher markups. When peak ends, the lower-boundary wins and the range shifts back down.\n- **Adjustment to initial range** — when the starting hypothesis turned out not optimal\n\n**Trigger**: A boundary arm is the winner\n\n- After iteration has been enabled for [Z] days, start signal check on a daily basis\n- Everyday, check in the past [X] days, if one boundary arm got over [N%] traffic in over [Y] days\n\n**Action**: Iterate the arms in winner direction\n\n- Move pricing window by 1 step size [S%], trigger notification email to PIC\n- If the new arm hits lower or upper pricing boundary, stop moving, trigger warning email to PIC, and keep current arms\n\n<table><colgroup><col/><col/><col/><col/></colgroup><tbody><tr><td colspan=\"4\"><b>Example</b><br/>Set up a new MAB with initial arms = {+3%, +4%, +5%}.
